📚 REGISTRATION & ADMISSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: How do I register at the faculty?
A: Registration can be done online or in person at the registration office (Office 101, Building A). Steps:
   1. Fill out the online registration form
   2. Submit the complete file (diploma copies, photos, ID card, transcripts)
   3. Pay tuition fees (1st installment)
   4. Receive your student card and platform access

Q: What documents are required for registration?
A: The registration file includes:
   - 2 recent passport photos
   - Copy of your national ID card (or passport for international students)
   - Certified copy of high school diploma
   - Transcripts from previous years
   - Completed and signed registration form
   - Tuition fee payment receipt

Q: What are the tuition fees?
A: Tuition fees vary by program:
   - Bachelor's (all majors): 4,500 DT / year
   - Master's: 5,500 DT / year
   - Payment can be made in 3 installments:
     • 1st installment: September (40%)
     • 2nd installment: January (30%)
     • 3rd installment: April (30%)
   - 10% discount for full payment at registration
   - Excellence scholarships available for top students

Q: What are the registration deadlines?
A: Registration calendar:
   - Bachelor's Year 1: July 1 — September 30
   - Re-enrollment Bachelor's 2/3: September 1-30
   - Master's registration: October 1-15
   - Late registration (with 15% surcharge): until October 31

Q: Can I transfer from another university?
A: Yes, transfers are possible under conditions:
   - Written request to the dean with transfer reason
   - Complete academic file from the original university
   - Minimum GPA of 10/20
   - Validation committee decides within 15 days

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
📄 ADMINISTRATIVE DOCUMENTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: How do I get an enrollment certificate?
A: You can get an enrollment certificate two ways:
   1. Via the chatbot: type "enrollment certificate" and fill out the form
   2. At the secretary's office: Building A, Ground Floor — 24-48h processing
   The certificate is free for enrolled students.

Q: How do I get a transcript?
A: The transcript is available:
   - Online via your student portal after results are published
   - At the secretary's office on request (48h processing)
   - The official stamped transcript is only available at the secretary's office

Q: How do I get a recommendation letter?
A: To get a recommendation letter:
   1. Send a written request to the professor
   2. Attach your CV and transcripts
   3. Specify the purpose (internship, master's, employment)
   4. Usual processing time: 1 week

Q: What documents can the chatbot generate?
A: The chatbot can automatically generate:
   - Enrollment certificate (PDF and Word)
   - Work certificate (for staff)
   - Residence certificate
   - Registration certificate
   All documents are pre-filled with your information.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
📅 TIMETABLE & EXAMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: Where can I check my timetable?
A: Your timetable is available:
   - On the "Planning" page of this application
   - Displayed on the main hall notice boards
   - Sent by email at the beginning of each semester
   Select your program to see your schedule.

Q: How do exams work?
A: The exam system includes:
   - Continuous assessment: 40% of the final grade (assignments, projects, participation)
   - Midterm exam: mid-semester (20%)
   - Final exam: end of semester (40%)
   - Retake session: 2 weeks after the main session
   The exam calendar is published 3 weeks before the session.

Q: What is the minimum grade to pass a module?
A: The minimum passing grade is 10/20. Advancement conditions:
   - Overall GPA ≥ 10/20
   - No eliminating grades (< 5/20)
   - Validation of all mandatory modules
   - Retake is available for failed modules

Q: What happens if I'm absent from an exam?
A: In case of exam absence:
   - Justified (illness with medical certificate): automatic retake access
   - Unjustified: grade of 0/20 assigned
   - Absence must be reported within 48h to the secretary
   - Medical certificate must be submitted within 72h

🎓 PROGRAMS & MAJORS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: What programs are offered?
A: Our faculty offers the following programs:

   BACHELOR'S (3 years):
   • Computer Science (CS)
   • Software Engineering (SE)
   • Information Systems (IS)
   • Networks & Telecommunications (NT)
   • Management Sciences (MS)
   • Digital Marketing (DM)

   MASTER'S (2 years):
   • Artificial Intelligence & Data Science (IADS)
   • Cybersecurity (CS)
   • Management Information Systems (MIS)
   • Finance & Accounting (FA)

Q: Can I change my program during the year?
A: Program changes are possible:
   - Only during the 1st month of the 1st semester
   - With approval from the program coordinator
   - Subject to available spots
   - Change form available at the secretary's office

Q: What are the required internships?
A: Internships are mandatory at certain levels:
   - Bachelor's Year 2: Observation internship (1 month — Summer)
   - Bachelor's Year 3: Final year project (2 months — FYP)
   - Master's Year 2: Professional internship (4-6 months — Thesis)
   The internship office (Office 203) helps students find placements.
